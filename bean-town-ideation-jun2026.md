# Bean Town — Ideation Session Notes
**Date:** June 2026 | **With:** Adam Smylie

---

## Core Thesis

> *"If the idea exists clearly enough, the physical thing becomes obvious."*

Bean Town starts as an idea, not a business plan. The ultimate customer is the founder — you know exactly what the experience needs to feel like. Define that clearly enough, and everything else (space, branding, product) follows.

**Central value: Appreciation of the bean — and the people who grow it.**

Not appreciation in the specialty-coffee-nerd sense (lightest, funkiest, highest-rated). Appreciation as in: most people have never been to Ethiopia or Colombia, never walked a coffee farm, have no idea how many cups come from one tree or how many steps separate cherry from cup. Bean Town bridges that gap without being preachy or inaccessible.

## The Experience — Key Ideas

### Entry ritual
- Remove shoes at the entrance — you're entering a different kind of space
- Deliberate slow-down signal; echoes Islamic mosque etiquette (respect the space)
- Walk barefoot through coffee beans on the floor (texture, smell, tactility — the growers' experience translated)

### No takeaway
- Sit-down only. The experience can't be rushed or containerised.

### The bar as a journey
- Walk *along* the bar as a path through the coffee lifecycle
- Screens showing: coffee on the tree → picking → processing (washed/natural/fermentation) → milling → roasting → brewing
- Inspired by Finca La Azotea (Guatemala, Antigua) — their farm walk compressed into an interior experience
- Replace jargon bag labels with *story*

### Bean display — greengrocer model
- Coffee beans organised by country of origin like fruit in a market — bananas with bananas, apples with apples
- Tactile, visual, navigable — not shelved and labelled with meaningless scores
- Possibility: church-chapel layout where each "origin" has its own alcove/station

## The Hierarchy — "The Smylie Hierarchy of Coffee Needs"

Sketched as a triangle (Illuminati-adjacent, noted):

```
        [THE BEAN]
       /  + people  \
      [  APPRECIATION ]
     [    EMOTION      ]
    [    CONNECTION     ]
   [     COMMUNITY       ]
  [        VALUES          ]
```

**Values at the base** — without brand values, growth pulls you off-course. Everything has to be built on these and stay aligned through scale.

**Community near the top** — community is what brings people with shared values together. But the magazine/merch layer only resonates with people *already* invested. The funnel starts with values.

## The Religious / Sacred Space Metaphor

Strong thread — and worth developing seriously:

- Cathedral layout: central nave = the bean's journey; side chapels = origin countries / growing cultures
- The growers as saints — stories, faces, places on display
- The Ethiopia origin myth (the discovery of coffee) = the creation story
- Aztec pyramid version also floated — the bean at the apex; the process of cultivation/sacrifice beneath it
- *"The sacrifice is a Starbucks corporate officer"* — noted for illustration brief
- Starbucks's own logo is a person (the Siren) — Bean Town inverts this: the logo is the bean, but the people/growers radiate out from it

The illustration style: something between sacred iconography and Aztec/pre-Columbian graphic art. Not ironic — genuinely reverent.

## Community & Membership

- **Waiting list with approval process** — not open to everyone; you apply to join
- Interview-style vetting (informal, but intentional) — aligns to the values-first model
- Brings in the right people, creates genuine exclusivity without being elitist about coffee knowledge

## The Magazine

- Annual issue, ~10 pages
- Combines travel photography (your photos, Adam's) from coffee-origin trips
- Short interviews — farmers, roasters, growers
- AI-generated layout + custom visuals
- Not a marketing tool — more like an artifact for people already inside the community
- Pairs with merch (cups, related items) — possible collab with other creators

## Cultural References That Shaped the Session

- **James Hoffmann** — on coffee being extraordinarily complex and still cheap relative to its production; the appreciation gap
- **Finca La Azotea** (Antigua, Guatemala) — the farm walk as experiential model
- **Lawson Japan / convenience coffee** — appreciation doesn't have to mean specialty orthodoxy; culture and context shape taste
- **Michelin restaurants** — technical excellence ≠ the only valid form of deliciousness
- **Islamic mosques** — the remove-your-shoes ritual; entering sacred space
- **Catholic cathedrals / chapels** — spatial model for origin stories
- **Aztec pyramids** — visual/iconographic language for the brand
