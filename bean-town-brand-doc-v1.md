# Bean Town — Brand Definition v1

*Co-founders: Alex Hamilton + Adam Smylie*
*Status: Initial brief, June 2026*

---

## The name

**Bean Town** — inspired by Vulfpeck's *Dean Town*. That's the energy: exceptional craft executed with warmth, playfulness, and zero superiority complex. Vulfpeck are world-class musicians who play like they're having the best time. Bean Town is world-class coffee knowledge delivered the same way.

---

## What Bean Town is

A globally-minded coffee education brand for the individual drinker. Not a café, not a roaster, not a specialty retailer — a brand that teaches people how to enjoy coffee more, across cultures, without the gatekeeping.

The core idea: **coffee as a lens on place and culture**, not as an art form to be perfected in isolation.

---

## What Bean Town refuses to be

- A chain. No Starbucks energy, no scalable-coffee-brand ambition.
- Pretentious. Specialty coffee's worst habit is making people feel stupid for not knowing the difference between a washed and a natural process. Bean Town never does this.
- Exclusionary. The person who drinks instant at home and the person who has a Decent espresso machine are equally welcome.
- Technically obsessed for its own sake. Quality matters, but the obsession with extraction ratios and bloom times is the trap.

---

## The 4th wave — our definition

- **1st wave:** Coffee as commodity (Maxwell House, Nescafé).
- **2nd wave:** Coffee as experience (Starbucks, café culture).
- **3rd wave:** Coffee as craft — practitioners taking the art seriously, specialty grades, terroir, single origin, precise technique.
- **4th wave (Bean Town's definition):** Coffee as connection. We keep the quality. We drop the hierarchy. The focus shifts from the practitioner's execution to the consumer's understanding and enjoyment. Education that brings people *in*, not gatekeeping that keeps them *out*.

---

## The concept — coffee + country

The signature format: each "edition" or "feature" pairs a country's traditional coffee culture with its associated pastry or food culture.

**Example:**
- *México:* Café de olla (cinnamon, piloncillo, clay pot) + pan dulce (conchas, polvorones)
- *Ethiopia:* Traditional jebena ceremony coffee + injera or roasted barley snacks
- *Vietnam:* Cà phê trứng (egg coffee) or cà phê đá (iced, condensed milk) + bánh mì or bánh tiêu
- *Japan:* Kyoto-style drip or canned boss coffee culture + wagashi or melon pan
- *Northern Ireland:* Strong builder's tea-influenced coffee culture... we'll workshop this one

The point: **the pastry is not an afterthought**. The pairing is the content. You're learning about a country through what people actually drink and eat together. This is the editorial backbone — for Instagram, newsletter, site, and eventually podcast conversations.

## Audience

The Bean Town drinker:
- Curious, not already converted. They probably drink coffee every day but don't know much about where it comes from or why it tastes the way it does.
- Internationally minded — travels, or wants to. Interested in culture through food.
- Not afraid of trying something new, but needs to feel welcomed into it.
- Likely 25–40. Could be anywhere — Madrid, Tokyo, Belfast, Toronto, Mexico City.

**The friend they trust most for coffee advice** isn't the barista who makes them feel judged — it's the person who's been to these countries, had the drinks, and says "you have to try it like this, it makes so much more sense."

That person is Bean Town.

---

## Voice and tone

**Peer, not professor.** James Hoffmann is the reference for accessibility — he makes technical knowledge feel human. Bean Town is similar in spirit but even less technical, more traveller, more story-first.

- Warm, curious, dry when it calls for it
- "We've been drinking our way through this, here's what we found" not "here is the correct way to appreciate coffee"
- Occasional humour — both Alex and Adam bring that naturally
- Never talks down. Never assumes knowledge. Always assumes genuine interest.

## Visual direction

### Palette

**Primary:** Earthy, warm, grounded. Think the inside of a jute coffee sack, the colour of dried fig, the green of coffee plant leaves.

Proposed palette:
- **Olive/dark green** — primary brand colour. Not trendy sage, not forest green. Something closer to army canvas or aged glass. `~#3D4A2E` territory.
- **Off-white / natural cream** — primary background/type. Warm, not clinical white. `~#F2EDE3`
- **Terracotta/rust** — accent for warmth and country-specific heat. `~#B5501C`
- **Dark espresso brown** — for depth, type weight, grounding. `~#2A1F14`
- **Muted gold/ochre** — secondary accent, pastry warmth. `~#C8963E`

**Mood:** A well-worn travel journal. A market stall in Oaxaca. A specialist map shop. Not Instagrammable minimalism — something that feels like it's been places.

### Typography

**Logo:** Adam hand-draws it — the CREAM precedent. Should feel hand-done, slightly imperfect, characterful. Not groovy-retro bubble type like CREAM (that's snow culture). Think more: **brushwork, ink, slightly editorial**. Could reference: zine culture, Japanese coffee shop signage, the handwritten chalkboard of a good independent café — but elevated.

Suggested direction for Adam: single-weight brush lettering, all lowercase or all caps (not mixed), with a slightly organic baseline — the letters should feel like they were written by someone who cares about craft but isn't trying to show off.

**Body / supporting type:** A humanist sans-serif for digital — approachable, warm, slightly characterful. Reference options:
- *Founders Grotesk* (editorial warmth)
- *Söhne* (intellectual but human)
- *Aktiv Grotesk* (clean but not cold)
- Free alternative: *DM Sans* or *Plus Jakarta Sans*

For newsletter/long-form: pair with a serif for body text — *Lora*, *Playfair Display*, or *Source Serif* work well with the earthy palette.

### Logo mark

Alongside Adam's hand-lettered wordmark, consider a simple icon:
- A coffee bean + map pin hybrid
- A simplified jebena or clay pot silhouette
- A coffee plant leaf
- A globe with a steam trail

Keep it monochromatic — works in single colour on sticker, embroidery, stamp.

## Content pillars (Instagram + newsletter)

1. **Country editions** — the signature format. One country, one traditional coffee preparation, one pastry pairing. Origin story, how to make it, where to try it.

2. **The daily cup** — informal, personal. What we're actually drinking right now, where we are, what it tastes like. Keeps the account human and current.

3. **Demystified** — single-concept education posts. What is a natural process? Why does altitude matter? What's the actual difference between arabica and robusta? Short, clear, zero condescension.

4. **Café visits** — when we're somewhere new, we find the interesting local coffee spots. Not listicles. A single place, why it's worth going, what to order.
