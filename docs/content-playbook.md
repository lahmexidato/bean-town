# Bean Town — Content Playbook

*Editorial strategy, content formats, and publishing guidance. Last updated: June 2026.*

---

## The editorial spine

Every piece of Bean Town content answers one or both of these questions:

1. **Why does this cup taste like this, here?**
2. **What does that tell us about the people who made it?**

Not: how to extract better. Not: which bean scores highest. Culture, not craft.

---

## Content pillars

### 1. Country editions *(signature format)*

One country. One traditional coffee preparation. One pastry or food pairing. 

**Structure:**
- Origin story — how did this preparation emerge? What is the cultural context?
- The preparation — how it's made, what's in it, what makes it distinct
- The pairing — why this food, why alongside this coffee, what the combination tells you
- Where to find it / how to make it at home (practical, not prescriptive)

**Length:** 800–1,200 words for Substack. 5–8 slides for Instagram carousel. 3 cards (teaser) on website.

**Voice check:** Would a friend who'd been there say this? If it reads like a textbook or a tasting note, rewrite it.

### 2. The daily cup *(informal, personal)*

What we're actually drinking right now. Where we are. What it tastes like. Keeps the account human.

**Format:** Single Instagram image or story. 1–3 sentences. No pressure to educate.

**Cadence:** 2–3 per week. Informal. Can be written in 10 minutes.

---

### 3. Demystified *(single-concept education)*

One concept, clearly explained. Zero condescension.

**Topics:**
- What is a natural process vs. washed vs. honey?
- Why does altitude matter?
- What's actually the difference between arabica and robusta?
- Why does Italian espresso taste different to third-wave espresso?
- What is a cortado? (And why is it different in Madrid vs. New York?)
- Why is robusta not inferior — just different?

**Format:** Single carousel (5–8 slides) or short Substack piece. One question, answered fully.

---

### 4. Café visits *(place-based)*

One café. Why it's worth going. What to order. Not a listicle — a single well-chosen recommendation.

**Who owns this:** Adam (Japan, Edinburgh, NZ), Alex (Madrid, Mexico City, Amsterdam)

---

## Content differentiation — the anti-pretension test

Before publishing anything, ask:
- Does this make the reader feel more knowledgeable or more ignorant?
- Does this explain the "why" or just state the "what"?
- Is this content treating robusta, instant, and condensed milk as legitimate choices in their cultural context?

If the answer to any of these is wrong, rewrite it.

- Also: essays, demystified deep-dives, café visit write-ups

## Instagram carousel template (country edition)

**Slide 1 — Cover:**
- Country name (small, uppercase, tracked, warm gray)
- Preparation name (large, DM Sans 500, cream)
- Pairing (Lora italic, terracotta)
- Background: espresso `#2A1F14`

**Slide 2–5 — Content:**
- Alternating cream and espresso backgrounds
- Olive left border accent
- Body text: DM Sans 400, 16px, generous leading
- Pull quotes: Lora italic, terracotta or ochre

**Slide 6 — CTA:**
- "Read the full edition at Bean Town →"
- Substack link in bio
- "bean town" wordmark, bottom right

| Format | Tone register | Length | Example opener |
|---|---|---|---|

| Daily cup | Personal, offhand | 1–3 sentences | "Third cortado of the day. La Latina. No complaints." |
| Café visit | Specific, editorial | 200–400w | "Pica in Belfast does one thing and does it right." |

## The Vulfpeck principle (north star for all content)

> Vulfpeck are world-class musicians who play like they're having the best time. *Dean Town* is technically extraordinary and sounds like a party. Bean Town aspires to this: genuinely excellent coffee knowledge, delivered like you're at a party with someone who happens to know everything about it.

If the content feels like work to read, it needs rewriting.

---

## What we don't cover (anti-brief)

- Equipment reviews (not the angle)
- Extraction ratios and brew parameters (third wave territory, not ours)
- Coffee scoring / cupping notes (practitioner frame, not consumer frame)
- Brand partnerships or affiliate links (not yet — maintain independence)
- "Best of" listicles (too surface level)
