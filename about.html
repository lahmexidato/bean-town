about us

Two friends from Northern Ireland. A lot of coffee. No barista credentials.

"We kept noticing the same thing: the way people drink coffee tells you something true about the place they're from."

The clay pot and piloncillo in a Mexican kitchen. The three-round jebena ceremony in Ethiopia. The canned coffee from a vending machine outside a ski lodge in Hokkaido. None of it needed to be evaluated against a cupping score to be interesting.

So we made a brand to explore that. We're not baristas, not roasters, not World Barista Champions. We're two people who travel, drink coffee, and think the interesting questions aren't about extraction ratios — they're about why this cup tastes like this, here, and what that means about the people who made it.

We're calling it the 4th wave. Less about perfecting the craft, more about understanding the culture. First round's on us.

co-founder

Alex Hamilton

Madrid (La Latina) — Irish-Mexican

Alex comes at coffee as a curious non-expert who has lived in enough places to notice that it means something different everywhere. Madrid daily life is a cortado at the bar, café con leche while standing up. Mexico City is family roots and café de olla before anything else happens in the morning.

Background in data storytelling and editorial work — which means Bean Town gets the "here's what's actually interesting about this" framing. Not the barista's view. The traveller with good instincts and a notebook.

Also: Northern Ireland, Amsterdam, Edinburgh, Paris, New York, and a running list of places where he ordered something and wrote it down.

co-founder

Adam Smylie

Edinburgh / Furano, Japan

Adam is a professional snowboard instructor trainer who runs CREAM Snowboarding — a small brand he built from scratch with a hand-drawn logo, merch, and a following. Bean Town's visual identity is his doing too.

He spends every winter in Japan, which gives him genuine first-person access to kissaten culture, canned coffee from vending machines at 6am, and the precise quiet of a Tokyo pour-over bar. He's also been to Vietnam, Nepal, and New Zealand — he writes from inside the experience, not just passing through it.

Bean Town's energy comes from a principle Adam brought in from the start: exceptional craft, zero self-seriousness. Same reason we named it after a Vulfpeck track.

the four waves

  • First wave — coffee as commodity. Maxwell House, Nescafé, the tin in the cupboard. Coffee as functional, not cultural. It gets you through the morning.
  • Second wave — coffee as experience. Starbucks, the Italian café ideal, the naming of the drinks. Coffee as occasion — somewhere to be, something to order.
  • Third wave — coffee as craft. Specialty grades, single origin, precise technique, terroir conversations. The practitioner's art form. The barista as auteur.
  • Fourth wave (our definition) — coffee as connection. We keep the quality. We drop the hierarchy. The focus shifts from the practitioner's execution to the drinker's understanding and enjoyment. Education that brings people in, not gatekeeping that keeps them out.

That's where Bean Town lives. Third wave quality, first round friendliness.
