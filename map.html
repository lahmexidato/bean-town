🗺️ REGION DE PRODUCCION

Chiapas

~40% of national production

Grown in the high-altitude volcanic soils bordering Guatemala, this represents Mexico's most robust and abundant coffee-growing terroir, shielded by dense rainforest canopies.

Elevation 1,200 – 1,750 meters
Avg. Temperature 18°C – 22°C
Annual Rainfall 1,800 – 3,000 mm
Soil Composition Humus-rich Volcanic Clay
Varieties Typica, Bourbon, Maragogype, Arábica
Find This Coffee
Maya Vinic cooperative (500 farming families, San Cristóbal area), Higher Grounds Trading · CESMACH cooperative (El Triunfo Biosphere Reserve), Black Coffee Roasting Co. · FESICH/FEJCEM Cafe Mujeres, OZO Coffee Roasters
🗺️ REGION DE PRODUCCION

Oaxaca

~30% of national production

Cultivated on the steep Pacific-facing slopes of the Sierra Madre del Sur, yielding coffees of exceptional clarity and sweetness, defined by the isolated Pluma Hidalgo cultivar.

Elevation 900 – 1,650 meters
Avg. Temperature 17°C – 23°C
Annual Rainfall 1,400 – 2,200 mm
Soil Composition Mineral-rich Clay Loam
Varieties Typica, Bourbon, Pluma Hidalgo
Find This Coffee
Pluma Hidalgo (Denominación de Origen, Zapotec smallholders), CaffeUmbria · Finca Las Nieves (1st place, 2024 Cup of Excellence) · Sierra Mazateca producers, Nordic Roasting Co.
🗺️ REGION DE PRODUCCION

Veracruz

~10% of national production

Mexico's oldest coffee region, framing the Gulf of Mexico. Cool oceanic winds and volcanic soil create a gentle, slow-maturing climate that fosters deep sweetness.

Elevation 1,100 – 1,400 meters
Avg. Temperature 15°C – 21°C
Annual Rainfall 1,500 – 2,500 mm
Soil Composition Limestone & Deep Volcanic Ash
Varieties Typica, Bourbon, Garnica, Mundo Novo
Find This Coffee
Las Lomas Community, Coatepec, Jaunt Coffee Roasters · Finca Cafetal El Equimite, biodynamic (7th, 2022 Cup of Excellence) · Finca El Estribo, Zongolica (8th, 2022 Cup of Excellence)
🗺️ REGION DE PRODUCCION

Puebla

~11% of national production

Nestled in Central Mexico's highland volcanic valleys. The farms here are small shade-grown plots that benefit from misty mountainous microclimates.

Elevation 1,000 – 1,500 meters
Avg. Temperature 16°C – 22°C
Annual Rainfall 1,600 – 2,200 mm
Soil Composition Volcanic Alluvial Soils
Varieties Typica, Bourbon, Caturra
Find This Coffee
Director al Origen (top 10, Cup of Excellence) · Sierra Norte shade-grown cooperatives · Cup of Excellence 2024 entrants from Puebla
🗺️ REGION DE PRODUCCION

Guerrero

~3% of national production

A rugged, remote region stretching into the southern highlands. Scarcity and traditional organic practices make these heavy-bodied coffees rare and distinctive.

Elevation 900 – 1,350 meters
Avg. Temperature 19°C – 25°C
Annual Rainfall 1,300 – 1,900 mm
Soil Composition Sandy Clay with Limestone
Varieties Typica, Bourbon
Find This Coffee
Crema.co Mexico origins page, crema.co/countries/mexico · Emerging market; sourcing improving via specialty importers
